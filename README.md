# Website Readiness Kit (Final)

*A 10–12 minute self-audit to make your website clearer, more credible, and conversion-ready.*

**Best for:** founders, startups, studios, service businesses\
**Outcome:** a readiness score + priority fixes + a launch checklist + reusable copy templates

## 1) Quick Start

1. **Score the 15 questions** in Section 2 (0 / 1 / 2).
2. **Calculate your total** in Section 3.
3. **Pick your Top Fixes** in Section 4.
4. Use the **Launch Checklist** (Section 5) and **Copy Templates** (Sections 7–8) to update your pages.

### Scoring

* **0** = missing / unclear / not implemented
* **1** = partially true / inconsistent
* **2** = clear / consistent / implemented

## 2) Website Readiness Score (15 Questions)

### A) Clarity (Positioning + Offer)

**Q 1.** In the first 5 seconds, my home-page clearly states **who we help** and the **outcome** we deliver. (0/1/2)\
**Q 2.** My home-page has **one primary CTA** that matches my goal (e.g., "Book a call" or "Request a proposal"). (0/1/2)\
**Q 3.** I sell **2–4 packaged offers** (not a long list of tasks). (0/1/2)\
**Q 4.** Each offer is specific and includes **scope boundaries** (what's included/not included). (0/1/2)\
**Q 5.** My website makes it obvious **why someone should choose us** (a real differentiator), not generic claims. (0/1/2)

### B) Conversion (User Journey)

**Q 6.** A visitor can reach the primary CTA in **2 clicks or fewer** from any key page. (0/1/2)\
**Q 7.** Every offer has a dedicated service page with: **who it's for, what you get, time-line, and CTA**. (0/1/2)\
**Q 8.** I have a clear process page (steps + what I need from the client + what they receive). (0/1/2)\
**Q 9.** My contact flow is low-friction (short form and/or calendar link; no unnecessary fields). (0/1/2)\
**Q 10.** I reduce decision anxiety (FAQ, pricing range or "starting at," time-line expectations, and next steps). (0/1/2)

### C) Credibility (Proof + Trust)

**Q 11.** I show proof (case studies, results, testimonials, screen shots, clients, credentials) near CTAs. (0/1/2)\
**Q 12.** My About page answers: **why we exist + what we believe + how we work** (not just a biography). (0/1/2)\
**Q 13.** My site includes basic trust signals (real contact method, clear business identity, and policies where relevant). (0/1/2)

### D) Technical Minimum + Measurement

**Q 14.** The site is fast and mobile-friendly (readable typography, comfortable spacing, easy taps). (0/1/2)\
**Q 15.** I track key actions (at minimum: form submissions and/or booking clicks, plus primary CTA clicks). (0/1/2)

## 3) Calculate Your Score + What It Means

**Total score:** \_\_\_\_ / 30

### Score Levels

* **0–14 — Not Ready**
  * Fix clarity and structure before polishing visuals.
* **15–22 — Nearly Ready**
  * Your foundation exists. Tighten your offer, proof, and CTA path.
* **23–30 — Ready**
  * Launch or scale confidently. Optimize based on real data.

### Fast Diagnosis (circle one)

* Biggest issue: **Clarity / Conversion Path / Proof / Copy / Technical / Tracking**
* Biggest opportunity: **Packaging / Stronger CTAs / Better proof / Better service pages / Faster site**

## 5) Launch Checklist (Prioritized)

### Must-have before launch (conversion essentials)

* \[ ] Clear "who + outcome" above the fold on the home-page
* \[ ] One primary CTA across key pages
* \[ ] Simple navigation: Home / About / Services / Process / Contact
* \[ ] 2–4 packaged offers (named + positioned)
* \[ ] Strong service page for each offer: who it's for, what you get, time-line, CTA
* \[ ] Proof near CTAs (testimonials, results, case snapshot)
* \[ ] Process page (steps + deliverables + what you need from the client)
* \[ ] Contact method works end-to-end (submission + confirmation + routing)
* \[ ] Basic SEO: page titles + meta descriptions + one H1 per page
* \[ ] Mobile check (readability + spacing + button size)
* \[ ] Analytics installed + conversion tracking for the primary CTA

### Important in the first 7 days (trust + decision support)

* \[ ] Publish 1–2 mini case studies (even small ones)
* \[ ] Add FAQs for each offer (time-line, range, scope, requirements)
* \[ ] Add "What happens after you contact us?" section
* \[ ] Add a lead capture option (resource / newsletter) if relevant
* \[ ] Add a "Start here" section for first-time visitors

### Next 30 days (optimization)

* \[ ] Publish 3–5 insight pages targeting real search questions
* \[ ] Upgrade proof: before/after, metrics, screen shots, client quotes
* \[ ] A/B test home-page headline + CTA text
* \[ ] Improve speed / Core Web Vitals
* \[ ] Add a comparison page ("Which offer fits?") if you have multiple packages

## 6) CTA Map (Where conversion happens)

### Choose ONE primary action

* Home-page hero (above the fold)

## 7) Swipe File (Plug-and-Play Copy)

Replace brackets with your specifics.

### A) Home-page headline formulas

1. **Outcome + Audience**
   * "We help \[audience] achieve \[outcome] without \[pain]."
2. **Transformation**
   * "From \[current state] to \[desired state] — with a website built to convert."
3. **Positioning**
   * "\[Brand] is a \[type of studio/team] building \[deliverable] for \[audience] who
     care about \[value]."
4. **Proof-led**
   * "Websites that turn clarity into action — backed by \[proof type]."

### B) Sub-headline templates

* "Strategy-led, content-first, and built for real decisions — not just
  aesthetics."
* "Clear offer. Clear story. Clear path to action."
* "Designed to earn trust fast: positioning, messaging, and conversion structure."

### C) CTA button options

#### Primary

* "Book a call"
* "Request a proposal"
* "Get a quote"
* "Start your project"
* "Apply to work together"

#### Secondary

* "See our process"
* "Explore services"
* "View work"
* "Read a case study"

### D) Proof block templates

#### Short

* "Trusted by \[type of clients] across \[region/industry]."

#### Testimonial frame

* "Problem: \_\_\_\_"
* "What we did: \_\_\_\_"
* "Result: \_\_\_\_"
* "Quote: '\_\_\_\_' — Name, Title"

### E) Offer description templates (value > features)

* "You'll leave with \[deliverable] so you can \[business outcome]."
* "Designed for \[audience] who need \[need] and want \[result]."
* "Not for: \[who it's not for]." (This increases trust.)

### F) FAQ templates (high-converting)

* "How long does it take?"
  * "Typical time-line is \[X–Y]. It depends on \[variables]."
* "What does it cost?"
  * "Most projects fall between \[range], depending on \[scope]."
* "What do you need from me?"
  * "We'll need \[assets], \[access], and a weekly check-in."
* "What happens after I contact you?"
  * "We respond within \[time], then \[next step]."

### G) Contact page microcopy

* "We reply within \[X-Y] hours."
* "Not sure which offer fits? Start with: \[entry option]."
* "Prefer email? \[email] | Prefer a call? \[calendar link]"

## 8) Page-by-Page Content Structure (Copy Outline)

### Home-page (recommended sections)

1. Hero: headline + sub-headline + primary CTA
2. Proof snapshot (logos/testimonial/metrics)
3. What you do (2–4 offers)
4. Why you (differentiators)
5. Process preview (3–5 steps)
6. Featured proof (case snapshot)
7. FAQ (3–6 questions)
8. Final CTA

### About page (recommended sections)

1. What you believe (principles)
2. Why you exist (mission, in plain language)
3. How you work (method)
4. What you value (quality, clarity, craft)
5. CTA to Services or Contact

### Service page (use for each offer)

1. Who it's for + problem statement
2. Outcome promise (what changes)
3. What's included (deliverables)
4. Time-line + collaboration model
5. Proof (testimonial, mini case, sample artifacts)
6. FAQ
7. CTA

### Process page

* Step 1: Discovery (inputs you need)
* Step 2: Strategy + structure
* Step 3: Design system
* Step 4: Development + QA
* Step 5: Launch + hand-over\
  Include: what the client provides + what they receive.

### Work / Case studies

Use 1–3 mini cases rather than a giant portfolio.

#### Mini case format

* Client / context
* Goal
* Constraints
* What we did
* Outcome (quantitative or qualitative)
* 1–2 visuals / artifacts

### Contact page

* Who should contact you (and for what)
* Form (minimal fields)
* Response time
* What happens next
* Alternative CTA (if they're not ready yet)

## 9) Minimum Measurement Setup (Tracking)

Track only what matters at first.

### Events to track (minimum)

* Primary CTA click
* Form submission success
* Calendar booking click (if used)
* Download (if relevant)

### Weekly review (10 minutes)

* Which pages get traffic?
* Which CTAs get clicks?
* Where do visitors drop off?
* What question do leads keep asking? (Add it to the FAQ.)
